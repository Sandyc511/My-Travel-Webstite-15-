History

Fushimi Inari Taisha Shrine, located in Kyoto, is one of Japan's most representative shrines, famous for its spectacular "Senbon Torii" (thousands of torii gates), attracting numerous tourists. Built in 711 AD, Fushimi Inari Taisha Shrine is dedicated to the god Inari. Inari is considered the deity who governs agriculture and commerce, so many merchants and businesses donate torii gates to pray for prosperity,forming the magnificent torii gate passageway we see today.

Attractive

Thousand Torii Gates: A spectacular tunnel formed by rows of red torii gates.Mount Inari Hiking Experience: Hike up Mount Inari, enjoying beautiful scenery along the way. Fox Statue: Symbolizing the messenger of Inari, a unique and distinctive feature. Open all day: You can visit at your own pace.

Food

Japanese cuisine is a unique and diverse style of cooking that is known for its emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients, delicate flavors, and beautiful presentation. It's one of the most popular and influential cuisines in the world, with a long and storied history that dates back centuries. Inari sushi (rice wrapped in tofu skin) Kyoto ramen (rich soy sauce broth) Japanese dango (traditional dessert) Matcha dessert (Kyoto specialty)

Transportation

Cóng dàbǎn chūfā kě chéng dā JR nàiliáng xiàn zhì `dào hé zhàn', chēchéng yuē 50 fēnzhōng, xiàchē hòu bùxíng jí kě dàodá. Huò chéng dā jīngbǎn diànchē zhì `fújiàn dào hé zhàn', bùxíng yuē 2 fēnzhōng jí kě dàodá shénshè. From Osaka, take the JR Nara Line to Inari Station (approximately 50 minutes).From there, it's a short walk to the shrine. Alternatively, take the Keihan Railway to Fushimi Inari Station (approximately 2 minutes walk).
